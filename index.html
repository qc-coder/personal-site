Bio

I am a quantum hardware physicist with over a decade of experience in superconducting circuits, circuit QED experiments, and qubit coherence. My work spans the full stack — from chip design and simulations to nanofabrication, cryogenic systems, and quantum measurements — with a focus on scalable, fault-tolerant quantum processors.

Over the past ten years, I have contributed to leading European quantum startups, including Alice & Bob and IQM Quantum Computers, where I led projects improving qubit coherence, reducing noise, and optimizing fabrication processes. During my PhD at CNRS Institut Néel, I developed cQED experiments that significantly enhanced qubit performance.

I am now based in Paris, establishing a company focused on producing high-coherence, scalable quantum processors for civil and public-domain applications.
